Instructions for Applicants:

Please answer these questions thoughtfully. Be sure to attach any relevant documents, including your resume, cover letter, and portfolio, before submitting your application.

This list of questions ensures that applicants provide a holistic view of their skills, experiences, and professional values, helping you find candidates who are the right fit for your guild.

Basic Information

1. Full Name

2. Email Address

3. Phone Number

4. Location (City, State, Country)

Professional Background

6. What is your highest level of education, and what did you study?

7. Do you have any certifications related to writing, editing, or publishing? (e.g., copyediting, technical writing, etc.)

8. How many years of professional writing or editing experience do you have?

9. Can you provide links to your online portfolio, published works, or writing samples? (Please include URLs.)

10. Which industries or topics do you specialize in? (e.g., B2B, B2C, finance, technology, lifestyle, etc.)

11. Are you comfortable working with technical or academic materials? Please explain.

Skills Assessment

12. Which types of editing are you experienced with? (Copyediting, Developmental Editing, Line Editing, Proofreading, Content Editing, Fact-Checking)

13. What writing or editing software/tools are you proficient in? (e.g., Microsoft Word, Google Docs, Grammarly, etc.)

14. Do you have experience with style guides such as APA, MLA, Chicago, or others? Which ones do you frequently use?

15. How do you approach ensuring clarity, tone, and consistency in your work?

16. Have you worked with SEO optimization in writing or editing? If yes, please explain your experience.

17. Describe your familiarity with CMS platforms (e.g., WordPress, Drupal).

Work Style and Availability

18. Are you seeking full-time, part-time, or freelance opportunities?

19. What is your availability? (e.g., hours per week, specific days, time zones.)

20. Are you comfortable working with deadlines and handling multiple projects simultaneously?

21. Do you prefer working independently or as part of a team?

22. How do you handle constructive feedback on your writing or editing?

Scenario-Based Questions

23. Describe a time when you had to edit a poorly written piece. How did you improve it, and what was the outcome?

24. Share an example of when you had to mediate between an author’s creative vision and the publisher’s requirements. How did you handle it?

25. Imagine you’re working on a tight deadline, and the material you receive needs significant revision. How would you approach the task?

26. How do you ensure the factual accuracy of the content you edit?

Cultural Fit

27. Why do you want to join the IIPE?

28. What do you value most about collaborative work in the publishing industry?

29. How do you stay updated on trends and changes in the writing and editing industry?

Additional Questions

30. Are you proficient in any languages other than English? If yes, please list them.

31. Do you have experience working with international clients or publications?

32. Are you comfortable working in diverse and inclusive environments?

33. Is there anything else you'd like us to know about your skills, experiences, or aspirations?
